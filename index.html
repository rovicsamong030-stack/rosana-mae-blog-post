My Journey In ICT

In our ICT class, one of the things I often do is *write notes* during discussions. Writing notes helps me understand and remember the lessons more clearly. It allows me to review the topics easily, especially when we have activities or quizzes. By writing notes, I also develop good study habits and become more organized. It’s a simple but effective way to keep track of everything we learned in class.
Another important activity we did was *editing in GIMP*. GIMP is a photo editing software that helped me learn how to design and enhance images. We used it to create posters, edit IDs, and make digital artworks. At first, it was a bit challenging to use because of the different tools and layers, but as I practiced, I became more comfortable with it. Editing in GIMP improved my creativity and taught me how to use digital tools to express ideas visually.
Mail Merge Source is one of the lessons we learned in ICT that I really found useful. It is used in Microsoft Word to make many personalized documents like letters or certificates faster. The Mail Merge Source usually comes from an Excel file where we put the names, addresses, or other information of the people we want to include. When we connect the Excel file to Word, the data from the source automatically appears in the document. For example, if we make certificates, each person’s name will appear in their own copy without typing it one by one. For me, learning about Mail Merge Source was helpful because it saves time and makes our work more organized. I realized that technology really helps make tasks easier, especially in school and office work.
